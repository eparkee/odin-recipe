Tex-Mex Meatballs

  1. Prepare the Meatballs:
    1. In a large mixing bowl, combine ground beef, breadcrumbs, minced garlic, chopped onions, chopped cilantro, cumin, chili powder, salt, and black pepper.
    2. Using your hands, mix all the ingredients until well combined.
    3. Shape the mixture into small meatballs, about 1-2 inches in diameter.
    4. Place the meatballs on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  2. Cook the Meatballs:
    1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
    2. Bake the meatballs in the preheated oven for about 20-25 minutes or until they are cooked through and browned.
    3. Remove the meatballs from the oven and set them aside.
  3. Prepare the Sauce:
    1. In a saucepan, heat olive oil over medium heat.
    2. Add diced onions and minced garlic to the pan and cook until softened.
    3. Stir in crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, cumin, chili powder, salt, and sugar.
    4. Simmer the sauce for about 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  4. Combine Meatballs and Sauce:
    1. Add the cooked meatballs to the sauce and gently stir to coat them evenly.
    2. Continue simmering for an additional 5-10 minutes to let the meatballs absorb the flavors of the sauce.
  5. Serve and Enjoy:
    1. Transfer the Tex-Mex meatballs to a serving dish.
    2. Garnish with chopped fresh cilantro and grated cheese, if desired.
    3. Serve the meatballs hot as an appetizer or as a main course with rice or tortillas.
